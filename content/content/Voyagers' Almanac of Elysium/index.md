# [[content/Voyagers' Almanac of Elysium/Arcana\|Arcana]]
>[!abstract] The Nature of Magic and the Supernatural
>In Elysium, magical and fantastical things echo throughout almost every aspect of life. From the foundational esotericisms upholding the [[content/Voyagers' Almanac of Elysium/Bastions\|bastions]] to the radiance inherit to [[content/Voyagers' Almanac of Elysium/Divinity\|Symbols]], these supernatural phenomena are referred to as [[content/Voyagers' Almanac of Elysium/Arcana\|arcana]].
>
>Some common examples of major [[content/Voyagers' Almanac of Elysium/Arcana\|arcana]] include the scholastic study of thaumaturgists and wizards, the light conducted through faith in the ordained and [[content/Voyagers' Almanac of Elysium/Divinity\|Symbols]], or the deep-rooted beliefs and traditions upheld by generations of commonfolk. 
# [[content/Ancestries in Elysium/index\|Ancestries in Elysium]]
>[!abstract] The Nature of Races and Racists
> In Elysium, the races all live in relative harmony. Most intelligent races see each other as equals and many live in mixed communities. Those who are prejudiced against others are a minority of either evil or misinformed individuals.
> 
> Nevertheless, monstrous races, such as hobgoblins or lycanthropes, are often still regarded as dangerous. While some would still welcome them into their communities, they would keep their distance from them as they do so.
# [[content/Voyagers' Almanac of Elysium/Bastions\|Islands & Bastions]]
>[!abstract] The Nature of Bastions and the Sea
> In Elysium, the land is broken into floating islands that drift through the sky along distinct and often peculiar courses. Some islands maintain steady, cyclical paths, while others show no consistent pattern at all, and the force that keeps them naturally afloat is an active field of study.
> 
> Any sufficiently large landmass can be reasonably referred to as a [[content/Elysian Glossary/B/Bastion\|bastion]], regardless of if it’s natural or manmade. Some bastions, like the [[content/Elysian Glossary/B/Black Iron Clasp]] are simply known by a settlement upon them, especially if it’s the only settlement. Others are typically referred to by the names the locals have given them, such as the landmass making up the [[content/Elysian Glossary/E/Dawnwind Peninsula]] or an uninhabited speeding rock named [[content/Elysian Glossary/E/Elintrea’s Comet]].
# [[content/Voyagers' Almanac of Elysium/Divinity]]
>[!abstract] The Nature of Divinity and Symbols
> In Elysium, the concept of godhood differs *greatly* from the ones found in settings like the Forgotten Realms or Exandria. In those settings the gods are an immortal, active presence within their worlds. Whereas in Elysium the divine originate from mortals ascending to become a [[content/Voyagers' Almanac of Elysium/Divinity\|Symbol]] through embodying their domain.
> 
> While traditional fantasy gods have distinctly divided portfolios, the domains in [[content/Elysian Glossary/E/Elysium]] often overlap. Two [[content/Voyagers' Almanac of Elysium/Divinity\|Symbols]] may embody different interpretations of similar ideas, as ideas rarely belong to only a single perspective.
